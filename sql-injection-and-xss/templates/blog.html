The Australian Defence Force will support the NSW flood emergency efforts from Tuesday.

“Emergency Management Australia have received an official request for assistance from NSW,” a spokesman from the Prime Minister’s office said.

“The request is for two search and rescue helicopters operating out of the NSW south coast for 24-hour operations. ADF will be able to provide this support and will commence service tomorrow.”

Emergency Management Australia is a division of the Department of Home Affairs.

Earlier today, Prime Minister Scott Morrison said they expected a formal request to be made from the NSW government.

“We understand this is likely to be for recovery support and clean-up operations, including personnel, vehicles and machinery,” he said. “The Premier [Gladys Berejiklian] and I were discussing that over the weekend.”

He said the ADF was “readying itself for this task that still lays ahead”.

“Particularly in the recovery phase,” Mr Morrison said. “It is working closely with NSW Emergency Services to co-ordinate potential areas for assistance.”
